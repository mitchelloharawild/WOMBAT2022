Workshop Organised by the Monash Business Analytics Team (WOMBAT)

Spatio-temporal visualisation for natural disasters


In this tutorial, we will use data visualisations of weather and hazard forecasts to better understand the impacts of natural disaster events.

Kate Saunders is a lecturer at the Monash University. Her core research interests are problems in statistical climatology, with a specific interest in climate extremes and extreme value theory. Her PhD studies were in applications of extreme value theory for modelling rainfall extremes in Australia at the University of Melbourne.

  • Session 1: Visualisation of ensemble weather forecasts for extreme rainfall.
  • Session 2: Visualising predicted flood extents and essential services information.
  • Session 3: Visualising forecast uncertainty for extreme rainfall events.
Participants need a basic knowledge of R, ideally with familiarity with the topics covered in R for Data Science. You will also need to have R and RStudio installed on your computer.
